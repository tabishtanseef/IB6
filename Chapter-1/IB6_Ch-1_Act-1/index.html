Leaf and its Parts

Choose the correct options from the box and label the diagram of a leaf.


Leaf Base

It is the short, swollen bud-like structure that joins the leaf at the end of the petiole, to the stem.

Leaf stalk or Petiole

It is the short and thin part of the leaf that joins the leaf to the stem.

Axillary buds

It is a small bud that develops at the axil of the leaf.

Leaf blade

The flat, expanded and thin part of the leaf is called leaf blade or lamina.

Leaf apex

The tip of the leaf blade is called the leaf apex.

Leaf margin

The edge of the leaf blade is called the leaf margin.

Midrib

The thick structure running through the middle of the lead blade is called the midrib or main vein.

Veins

The smaller vein-like structure emerging from the midrib are called veins.
